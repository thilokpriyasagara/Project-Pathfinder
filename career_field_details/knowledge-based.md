# Knowledge-Based Professions

## Overview
Knowledge-Based professions are built on formal qualifications, legal recognition, and professional licensing. These careers are stable and respected, but they require longer training periods.

## Main pillars
### Medicine (MBBS)
- Requires Biology stream background
- Registration with SLMC is needed to practise

### Law (LLB / Attorney-at-Law)
- Can begin through state universities, Law College, or private LLB routes
- Professional qualification is needed to practise

### Chartered Accountancy / Finance
- Professional qualifications such as CA Sri Lanka, CIMA, or ACCA are key

## Study routes
### Law
- State university LLB
- Sri Lanka Law College entrance route
- Private LLB pathways

### Medicine
- State medical faculties
- KDU
- Foreign medical degree route followed by ERPM

## Entry summary
- Doctor: 3 S passes in Biology stream
- Lawyer: A/L qualifications plus Law College/LLB pathway requirements
- Chartered Accountant: usually begins with 3 S passes and professional study route

## Immediate steps
- Join entrance coaching for Law College if relevant
- Start a science foundation for private medical/allied health pathways
- Improve professional English
- Seek job shadowing or observation opportunities

## Suggested CTA
Choose a professional path with clear qualifications, licensing, and long-term career stability.
